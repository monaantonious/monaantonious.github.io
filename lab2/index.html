Plan Around Feedings and Programs

It's fun to see otters, penguins, and other exhibits anytime, but it's especially fun to see them at feeding time when the animals are sure to be active. Trainers will also share information about the animals during feedings. Check their daily schedule on the Monterey Bay Aquarium.

Grab a Visitor's Guide Map

The Aquarium's layout of the exhibits will help you plan how you'll go through the attractions. This map shows visitor centers, gift shops, restrooms, and points of interest on the Aquarium's two floors. Some of the popular exhibits to see on the first floor of Monterey Bay Aquarium are Kelp Forest, Touch Pools, Aviary, Octopus, Sea Otters, etc. The first floor also has a restaurant, a cafe, and a souvenir store. The Aquarium's second floor has Penguins corner, Splash Zone, Jelly Fish, The Open Sea Exhibit, Play Area for kids, etc. The upper floor also has one more Touch Pool.

Strategize Your Visit

Get to the Aquarium as close to opening time as possible. Prioritize the exhibits you most want to see first. These are the exhibits that you don't want to miss: Sea Otters: The Otter exhibit isn't large, but it's on two levels so that you can see otters under and out of the water Open Sea: This exhibit has an enormous tank filled with sharks, sea turtles, sunfish, sting ray, and more. Kelp Forest: This is another exhibit with lots of visual impacts. Look for leopard sharks, octopuses, and other fascinating creatures in this area. Rocky Shore: Don't miss the Wave Crash, where you can witness the power of 600 gallons of water crashing against the window every 30 seconds.
